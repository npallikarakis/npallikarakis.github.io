About

I am a PhD researcher in Applied Mathematics at the National Technical University of Athens (NTUA), specializing in inverse electromagnetic scattering problems and deep learning. My research focuses on developing neural networks for solving complex inverse problems in doubly-connected impedance obstacles, combining rigorous mathematical theory with practical computational methods.

With 7+ years of experience in semiconductor metrology and critical dimension measurement, I bring practical insights to computational physics. My work bridges theory, simulation, and machine learning applications, with expertise in numerical methods, signal processing, and scientific computing.

Research Focus

Inverse Electromagnetic Scattering

Shape reconstruction and material property determination from far-field measurements using advanced inverse problem techniques and machine learning approaches.

Deep Learning & Neural Networks

Designing specialized architectures (circular CNNs, neural operators) for solving PDEs and inverse problems with applications to electromagnetic wave propagation.

Numerical Methods

Development and analysis of numerical techniques for Maxwell transmission eigenvalue problems, Bessel function expansions, and scientific computing solutions.
